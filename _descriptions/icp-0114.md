---
title: Galway's Victoria
objectid: icp-0114
---

The Victoria Cinema opened on Galway's Victoria Place on a site formerly occupied by Cloran's warehouse on 2 August 1915 to coincide with the city's Race Week. Owned, designed and run until 1922 by Robert W. Mackie, it could hold 300 when it opened, but this appears to have rise to 588 by the time it was destroyed by fire in July 1926. 
Commenting on construction in advance of its opening, the Connacht Tribune noted that it "has a fire-proof floor...The entire building may practically be said to be fire-proof. The Cinema is enclosed in a six-inch concrete wall, with a trap door underneath, through which the operator can descend in case of fire, thus restricting the outbreak to the box." The cinema nevertheless burned to the ground on 24 July 1926 and was eventually replaced by W.P. Higgins's garage.
